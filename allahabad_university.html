allahabad university

The University of Allahabad is a Central University located in Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh. It was established on 23 September 1887 by an act of Parliament and is recognised as an Institute of National Importance (INI). It is the 4th oldest modern University in India.Its origins lie in the Muir Central College, named after Lt. Governor of North-Western Provinces Sir William Muir in 1873, who suggested the idea of a Central University at Allahabad, which later evolved to the present university. Its Central University status was re-established through the University of Allahabad Act 2005 by the Parliament of India.

The campus is spread around the city of Prayagraj across the area of Old Katra and Bank Road. It consists of major five faculties, namely Science, Arts, commerce, law, management faculties with the arts faculty housing the Administrative Block of Chatham Lines. It has numerous residential hostels for students.

Location: Senate House, University Road, Old Katra, Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh 211002

Timings: Monday through Friday from 8 AM to 5 PM

How to Reach

By Flight

The nearest airport is Prayagraj Airport, approximately 14 km from Allahabad University. Prayagraj Airport is well connected by railway networks with most of the cities of India with daily flights. From the airport, travelers can hire a taxi or use app-based cab services to reach the site.

By Train

Allahabad Railway Junction is about 4 km from Allahabad University. It is well connected by railway networks with most of the cities of India. Trains to and from Allahabad Junction Railway Station are frequent. Upon arrival, visitors can hire a taxi or use app-based cab services to reach the site.

By Bus

The Civil Lines Bus Stand is around 5 km from Allahabad University. State-run buses and private operators connect Prayagraj to various cities. From the bus stand, local transport options like auto-rickshaws or app-based cab services are available to reach the destination.
