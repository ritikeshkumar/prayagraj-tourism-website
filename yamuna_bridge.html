new yamuna bridge

The New Yamuna Bridge is a cable-stayed bridge located in Prayagraj.[2][3][4] The bridge was constructed by the end of 2004 with the aim of minimizing the traffic over the Old Naini Bridge.[5] The bridge runs north–south across the Yamuna River connecting the city of Prayagraj to its neighborhood of Naini. The construction was consulted by COWI A/S, a Danish consulting company. Main construction was done by Hyundai and was successfully completed in 2004.

The total length of the bridge is 1,510 m with a longest span of 260 m which is supported by a cable which stays in a concrete anchor bridges.

The bridge offers stunning views of the Yamuna river as you cruise on it. You can also take pictures of the marvelous construction of the bridge.

Location: Allahabad-Naini

Timings: Open 24 hours

How to Reach

By Flight

The nearest airport is Prayagraj Airport, approximately 18 km from New Yamuna Bridge. Prayagraj Airport is well connected by railway networks with most of the cities of India with daily flights. From the airport, travelers can hire a taxi or use app-based cab services to reach the site.

By Train

Allahabad Railway Junction is about 10 km from New Yamuna Bridge. It is well connected by railway networks with most of the cities of India. Trains to and from Allahabad Junction Railway Station are frequent. Upon arrival, visitors can hire a taxi or use app-based cab services to reach the site.

By Bus

The Civil Lines Bus Stand is around 11 km from New Yamuna Bridge. State-run buses and private operators connect Prayagraj to various cities. From the bus stand, local transport options like auto-rickshaws or app-based cab services are available to reach the destination.
